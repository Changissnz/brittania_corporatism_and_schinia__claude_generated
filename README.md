Western corporatism had taken a Gothic turn since large-scale cloud computing, circa 2014. Physical businesses seemed to be dying, while internet businesses were booming. Individuals, especially the youth, took to the internet for an unhuman amount of needs, effectively replacing socializing and formal education. There was mismatch between glamours and ideals on the internet and the real world. And the real world was not doing as well as post-WW2 Western civility had wished to maintain. Where work needed to be done, workers begrudgingly went back to the grit of labor. Automation could not fully replace. Overproduction had become a problem. Not all scientific prototypes, despite their effectiveness, could be scaled up. Individuals took liberties to turn to less noble pursuits than orthodox professions.

This Gothicism occurred at a time when Schinia and its vassal states, despite world-renowned corruption, were scaling up its enterprises, building cities that rivaled the marvels of the famed NYC. New York City, although still an important financial city of the West, was not unchallenged in image and outreach to cities such as Arabia's Dubai and even Singapore. This is an observation of the globalized state of the world, where no man could really think of himself as a king of the hill when he was given media from other corners of the world showing him that he was, ultimately, an ant in the resources of labor.
